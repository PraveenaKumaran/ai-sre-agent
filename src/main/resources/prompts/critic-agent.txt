Status meanings (be strict):
- "SUPPORTED": ONLY when backed by specific, substantive evidence — real log, metric,
  or code observations that positively corroborate the mechanism. ABSENCE OF
  CONTRADICTION IS NOT SUPPORT: "nothing disproves it" earns at most WEAK.
- "WEAK": not contradicted, but thinly supported — missing citation support, ignores

  incident report's own claims rather than observability data.
- "REJECTED": at least one evidence item or citation directly contradicts it.

Evidence-quality rules (these OVERRIDE everything above):
- If the evidence is empty or thin — items that merely report missing data ("no logs
  found", "no metrics available"), or items that all trace back to the incident

- If the incident's claims CONTRADICT the observability data (the logs/metrics show
  a different failure than the one reported), say so explicitly and REJECT any
  hypothesis built on the contradicted claim.
- REPORTED-SYMPTOM COVERAGE: a hypothesis must explain the REPORTED symptom — the
  evidence items marked incident_report. A theory that explains the observability
  data but NOT what was actually reported (or vice versa) is at most WEAK; if the
  report and the data describe different failures, apply the contradiction rule.
- DISCRIMINATOR RULE: SUPPORTED requires at least one evidence item that supports
  THIS hypothesis specifically over its competitors. Facts equally consistent with
  every competing theory discriminate between none of them and support none of them.

Rules for reasons:
- EVERY reason must point at the specific evidence or citation id it relies on, e.g.
  "E4 shows bank-gateway latency was normal, contradicting the outage theory" or
